# FIFA World Cup 2026 - Group Stage
## Data Analysis

---

# Overview

FIFA World Cup 2026 is being co-hosted in **United States of America, Mexico, and Canada**. This is the **23rd FIFA World Cup**, and the first time ever with **48 teams**.

The tournament is being hosted across **16 different venues**:

- 11 in the United States
- 3 in Mexico
- 2 in Canada

The participating teams qualified through their respective continental qualifiers, while the three host nations received automatic qualification.

---

# Format

This is the first FIFA World Cup to feature **48 participating teams**.

The teams are divided into **12 groups of 4 teams each**.

- Win = **3 Points**
- Draw = **1 Point**
- Loss = **0 Points**

The **top two teams** from each group automatically qualify for the **Round of 32**, along with the **eight best third-placed teams**.

The knockout stage follows this format:

- Round of 32
- Round of 16
- Quarter Finals
- Semi Finals
- Final

# Analysis of Group Stage

## Overview

The group stage consisted of **72 matches**, producing **215 goals** across the tournament, which consisted **12 own goals**.

The tournament saw:

- **143 different goal scorers**
- **130 different assist providers**
- **Germany** recording the highest number of goal contributors (**10**)

A total of:

- **177 Yellow Cards**
- **10 Red Cards**

were shown during the group stage, exceeding the combined total of the previous two FIFA World Cups (**8**).

Out of the **215 goals**:

- 30 were Headers
- 8 were Penalties
- 5 were Free Kicks

The group stage saw significantly more goals during the **second half**, with:

- **120 goals (56%)** scored in the second half
- **95 goals (44%)** scored in the first half

The **latest goal** of the group stage was scored by **Marko Arnautović** in the **102nd minute**.

Interestingly, the **7th minute** produced the highest number of goals, with **7 goals** scored at that exact minute.

The Argentine legend **Lionel Messi** finished the group stage as the tournament's leading scorer with **6 goals**, followed by **Erling Haaland, Kylian Mbappé, Vinícius Jr., and Ousmane Dembélé**, each with **4 goals**.

---

# Team-wise Statistics

European nations dominated the attacking charts during the group stage, with **Germany, France, and the Netherlands** each scoring **10 goals**.

At the opposite end of the pitch, **Spain** and **Mexico** demonstrated remarkable defensive consistency by keeping **clean sheets in every group-stage match**, while **Iraq** and **Tunisia** conceded the highest number of goals (**12**), reflecting their defensive struggles.

The Dutch were the most efficient team in front of goal, recording the tournament's best **shot efficiency** with exactly **4.00 shots per goal**.

On the other hand, **Scotland** required **29 shots** to score just **one goal**, making them the least efficient attacking side.

**Panama** remained the only nation among the **48 participating teams** to finish the group stage without scoring a single goal and were consequently eliminated.

The Netherlands' efficiency was also reflected in their **Expected Goals (xG)** numbers. They overperformed their xG by **4.76**, scoring **10 goals** from an expected **5.24 goals**, followed by **France**, who overperformed their xG by **4.04**.

Conversely, **Turkey** endured a disappointing campaign and were eliminated despite generating quality opportunities. They underperformed their xG by **3.54**, scoring only **3 goals** despite recording **more than 70 shots** and generating an xG of **6.54**.

Turkey also finished alongside **England** with the highest number of **Big Chances Missed (9)**, clearly highlighting one of the primary reasons behind their early elimination.

---

# Player Performance

**Lionel Messi** once again lit up the FIFA World Cup. The two-time Golden Ball winner leads the **Golden Boot race** with **6 goals**, holding a **two-goal advantage** over **Kylian Mbappé**.

Speaking of Mbappé, he is continuing to build an extraordinary World Cup legacy. The French superstar has already contributed **6 goal involvements** during the group stage, registering **4 goals** and **2 assists**.

He has been supported brilliantly by **Michael Olise**, who leads the tournament's assist charts with **3 assists**, alongside **Bruno Guimarães** and **Alexander Isak**.

Olise has also created **7 chances** during the tournament.

The **Chances Created** chart, however, is led by **Leandro Trossard**, who has produced **13 chances**. Surprisingly, none of those opportunities have resulted in an assist, reflecting **Belgium's struggles in front of goal**, as they had to wait until their third group-stage match to score their first open-play goal.

Finishing also proved problematic for **Ecuador**, with striker **Enner Valencia** missing the highest number of **Big Chances (4)**.

From a dribbling perspective, **Ibrahim Maza** of **Algeria** completed the highest number of successful dribbles (**13**), followed by **Julio Enciso** (**11**) of **Paraguay**. Both players played a significant role in helping their respective nations qualify for the knockout stages.

Although **Uruguay** experienced a disappointing tournament overall, midfielder **Rodrigo Bentancur** emerged as one of the team's standout performers, recording **29 ball recoveries** across three matches—the highest total in the tournament. He was closely followed by **Granit Xhaka** of **Switzerland**, who recorded **24 recoveries**.

Meanwhile, Ecuador's **Pedro Vite** enjoyed an outstanding group stage, ranking among the tournament leaders in both **recoveries (23)** and **tackles (14)**. In addition to his defensive contributions, he also created **8 chances**, one of which resulted in a goal.

# Conclusion

The group stage of the FIFA World Cup 2026 showcased contrasting tactical philosophies, outstanding individual performances, and several statistical surprises.

While established footballing nations largely justified expectations, a number of emerging teams demonstrated that efficient finishing, defensive organisation, and disciplined performances can compensate for lower possession and fewer scoring opportunities.

As the tournament progresses into the knockout stage, these trends will continue to evolve. Future editions of this analysis will examine how tactical adjustments, player form, and statistical performance influence the race toward the FIFA World Cup trophy.
